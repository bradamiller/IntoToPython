The Stuck Robot

Demo: Robot drives to first intersection, stops...

Run track_until_cross() again... Nothing happens! The robot doesn't move!

Why? The robot is still ON the cross. Both sensors are high. It thinks it's already at an intersection!

"How do we tell the robot to keep going to the NEXT intersection?"

The Clearing Problem

After track_until_cross(), the robot is AT the cross

STUCK! Sensors on the cross
straight(8, 0.5) ➔
CLEARED! Wheels over the cross

If we call track_until_cross() again without clearing:

Both sensors are already over the cross line

is_at_cross() returns True immediately

The robot "detects" the intersection it's already on!

Solution: Drive forward 8 cm to move the turning center over the intersection.
3 / 9

Clearing the Intersection

The fix: Drive forward exactly 8 cm to align the turning center over the intersection

# Detect the intersection
tracker.track_until_cross()

# Clear: move turning center over the intersection
tracker.drivetrain.straight(8, 0.5)

# Now we can turn or detect the NEXT intersection
tracker.track_until_cross()

Why 8 cm?

8 cm = distance from front sensors to rear turning center

Positions the wheels directly over the intersection

Turns will rotate about the cross point

Driving Two Intersections

Complete program:

board.wait_for_button()

# Intersection 1
print("Driving to intersection 1...")
tracker.track_until_cross()
print("Reached intersection 1!")

# Clear: align turning center over intersection
tracker.drivetrain.straight(8, 0.5)

# Intersection 2
print("Driving to intersection 2...")
tracker.track_until_cross()
print("Reached intersection 2!")

# Clear again
tracker.drivetrain.straight(8, 0.5)

This works! But what about 5 intersections? 10?

Using a For Loop

intersections = 3

for i in range(intersections):
    print("Driving to intersection", i + 1)
    tracker.track_until_cross()
    print("Reached intersection", i + 1)

    # Always clear: align turning center over intersection
    tracker.drivetrain.straight(8, 0.5)

Why clear every time, even the last?

Clearing moves the turning center over the intersection — ready for a turn or the next move!

6 / 9

Making It a Function

Wrap it up in a reusable function:

def drive_intersections(tracker, count):
    for i in range(count):
        tracker.track_until_cross()
        tracker.drivetrain.straight(8, 0.5)

Usage:

drive_intersections(tracker, 2)   # Drive 2 intersections
drive_intersections(tracker, 4)   # Drive 4 intersections
This function will be very useful in the final project!

Debugging Tips

Robot skips an intersection? Check that the clearing distance (8 cm) is correct

Robot stops between intersections? Make sure straight(8, 0.5) isn't overshooting

Use print() statements to track which intersection the robot is at

Checkpoints

Does the robot stop at intersection 2 (not 1, not 3)?
Does the for loop version match the manual version?
Does drive_intersections(tracker, 3) stop at the 3rd intersection?
Does the function work with count = 1?
