Turning at an Intersection

The robot must be AT an intersection to turn.

Before turn_right()

After turn_right()

What turn_right() does:

Spins the robot 90° to the right

Stops when the sensors find the perpendicular line

The robot is now lined up and ready to drive again

After arriving at an intersection with drive_intersections(), you can turn or keep going — the robot's wheels are already in the right spot.

Combining Drives and Turns

Chain drive_intersections() and turns to navigate any path:

# Drive 2 intersections, turn right, drive 2 more
drive_intersections(tracker, 2)
tracker.turn_right()
drive_intersections(tracker, 2)

The pattern is simple:

drive_intersections() handles driving and arriving at intersections

turn_right() / turn_left() handle turning

Just alternate between driving and turning to follow any path

Every grid path is just a sequence of drives and turns!

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Turning when NOT at an intersection

# Wrong — robot is mid-line!
tracker.turn_right()
drive_intersections(tracker, 2)
# Correct — drive to intersection first
drive_intersections(tracker, 1)
tracker.turn_right()
drive_intersections(tracker, 2)

Mistake 2: Wrong turn direction

Left and right depend on the robot's current heading. Plan on paper first! Draw the robot facing the direction of travel, then determine left vs. right from its perspective.

When in doubt, draw it out! Paper plans prevent wrong turns.
